CSS Font Basics

Font families

font-family:

Serif

This font family has decorations on the tails of letters, making it more difficult to read.

Sans-serif

This font is generally preferred for most pages. It is easiest to read.

Default on most computers is Arial or Helvetica.

Monospace

Monospace is used to replicate typewriter documents and also used for code examples.

Cursive

Cursive is the most decorative, yet most difficult to read.

See a full list of PC and Compatable fonts here

Fantasy

Straight out of LOTR.

Specifying Font Names

You can also directly code in the font name you want displayed to override user's defaults.

p { font-family: Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;}

Output:

This will show Helvetica if you have it. If not, it will be in Arial or another general sans-serif font.

text-align:

p:first {text-align: left}

I am aligned to the left.

p:nth-child(3) {text-align: justify}

I am justified across the parent element. If there is a lot of text, it ill be evenly spaced across the element space and will not have jagged edges. Think of newspaper columns where all to words seem to end up right on the edge of the columns.

p:nth-child(2) {text-align: center}

I am aligned in the center.

p:first {text-align: last}

I am aligned to the right.

text-decoration:

This is commonly used to remove underlines from < a > tags.

a {text-decoration: none}
I am underlined
I am not underlined
